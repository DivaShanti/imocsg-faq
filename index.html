General ownership & warranty 5 FAQs ▾
What are the warranty coverages that came with my IM purchase in SG?Awaiting EK ▾
Your IM purchase in Singapore from EK (Eurokars EV) includes three distinct coverages:

  • New Car Warranty: 5 years or 150,000 km, whichever comes first.
  • Battery Warranty: 10 years or 200,000 km, whichever comes first.
  • Free Servicing: 10 years or 200,000 km, whichever comes first — covers all scheduled maintenance at the EK service centre.

A formal written warranty policy document has been requested by owners (IM-022) and is pending publication by EK. Always verify specifics directly with EK.
What is NOT covered by warranty — what counts as wear and tear?Manual-sourced ▾
The following are consumables and wear items not covered by the new car warranty per the IM manual:

  • Brake pads: minimum 2 mm usable thickness before replacement.
  • Brake discs: front 28–30 mm, rear 23–25 mm minimum.
  • Wiper blades: replace when rubber shows hardness, cracking, or streaking.
  • Cabin A/C filter: replace at each service interval.
  • Tyres: replace at the 1.6 mm tread wear indicator. Cold pressures — front: 250 kPa / 2.5 bar, rear: 280 kPa / 2.8 bar.
  • Smart key battery: CR2032 coin cell.
  • Brake fluid: DOT 4 — replace on schedule.
  • Windscreen washer fluid, coolant top-up, drive unit fluid — all consumables.

Any damage from misuse, unauthorised modification, or accidents is excluded from all warranty claims.

Charging — apps, networks & how-to 5 FAQs ▾
What connector type does my IM use and which chargers are compatible?Manual-sourced ▾
Your IM uses the CCS2 (Combined Charging System Type 2) connector for DC fast charging, and Type 2 for AC slow charging. CCS2 is the national standard in Singapore — compatible with the vast majority of public chargers.

IM charging specs per the manual:
  • DC fast charging (800V variants*): up to ~396 kW max — 20% to 80% in approximately 20 min on an 800V DC pile, 30 min on a 400V DC pile.
  • DC fast charging (400V/75kWh LFP variant): up to ~153 kW.
  • AC slow charging (11 kW): 100 kWh battery full charge approximately 11.5 hrs; 75 kWh approximately 8.5 hrs.
  • AC slow charging (7 kW): 100 kWh approximately 16.5 hrs; 75 kWh approximately 12.5 hrs.

* 800V fast charging only available on the 100 kWh Sport RWD and Performance AWD variants.
Which apps should I download for EV charging in Singapore? ▾
The essential apps for SG IM owners:

  • SP App (SP Mobility) — Singapore's largest fast-charging network with 3,300+ charging points at 800+ locations (HDBs, malls, offices). Scan QR to start, pay by card in-app. Also earns ChargeUP rewards. MOST IMPORTANT to have.
  • Shell Recharge — available at Shell service stations and malls like Suntec City. DC ~$0.83/kWh, AC ~$0.74/kWh. Download Shell Recharge Asia app.
  • Charge+ — growing network at commercial properties. Check charge.com.sg app.
  • CDG Engie — smaller network; useful as a backup.
  • PlugShare — community-sourced map showing all networks including real-time reviews of working chargers. Excellent for checking if a specific charger is functioning before you drive there.

Typical public DC fast charging rates: $0.75–$0.83/kWh. AC rates: $0.58–$0.74/kWh. Idle fees apply at most networks after a 15–30 min grace period once charging ends ($0.50/min up to $40 cap) — unplug promptly.
How do I use a public charger for the first time? Step-by-step. ▾
Step-by-step for first-time use (using SP App as example — process is similar for other apps):

1. Download the SP App and register an account. Link a credit/debit card.
2. In the app, tap Explore > EV Charging to see the map of nearby chargers. Filter by DC or AC as needed.
3. Drive to the charger. Check the connector type on the pillar matches CCS2 (for DC) or Type 2 (for AC).
4. Open your car's charging port (press the upper right area of the charging port door (it will automatically open), OR use the 3D vehicle model on the central screen [Manual-sourced]). Insert the connector firmly until it clicks and locks.
5. In the SP App, tap 'Scan QR' and scan the QR code on the charger pillar, or enter the serial number. Confirm the session starts in the app.
6. The car will show charging status on the centre screen and instrument cluster.
7. To stop: tap 'Stop' in the app and wait for the connector lock to release before unplugging. Move your car promptly to avoid idle fees.

Tip: Before driving to a charger, use PlugShare to check recent user reviews — it quickly reveals if a specific charger is out of order.
Does my IM support one-pedal driving? ▾
No — one-pedal driving (where regen braking is strong enough to bring the car to a complete stop without touching the brake pedal) is NOT available on the IM5 or IM6 as currently configured. This is a commonly raised complaint on global IM owner forums.

What IS available per the IM manual:
  • Three regen levels via Settings > Controls > ERS or Settings > Driving > ERS:
  • OFF: energy recovery disabled, coasting freely.
  • LOW: light regeneration, longer coast distance.
  • STANDARD: stronger regeneration, shorter coast distance.

STANDARD is the closest to one-pedal feel but does not bring the car to a complete halt on its own. A dedicated one-pedal mode has been raised as a feature request to IM Motors globally.
What is the best charging routine to maximise battery health long-term?Manual-sourced ▾
Based on the IM6 manual:

  • Equalisation charge: the manual recommends carrying out a slow AC charge to full (100%) every month to extend the service life of the high-voltage battery pack. This balances every battery cell so as to ensure overall battery performance. [Manual-sourced]
  • Long-term parking: charge to at least 50% SOC before leaving. Charge at least once every 3 months — keep above 50% on the instrument after each charge. [Manual-sourced]
  • NEVER leave the car parked for more than 7 days with a critically low battery (no valid driving range shown on the instrument) — this can permanently damage the battery cells. [Manual-sourced]
  • The manual recommends starting the vehicle and running it for at least half an hour every week to help extend the 12V battery service life. [Manual-sourced]
  • If the car has been parked for a long time before first use, perform a complete equalisation charge (slow AC charge to 100%) before returning to regular driving. [Manual-sourced]

Note: Keeping daily SOC between 20–80% is widely recommended general EV best practice but is NOT stated in the IM6 manual — apply at your own discretion.
Road trips — JB, KL & Penang 5 FAQs ▾
Can I drive my IM to JB / Malaysia? What do I need to sort out first? ▾
Yes — driving to Malaysia is straightforward with some preparation:

  • VEP (Vehicle Entry Permit): mandatory for all Singapore-registered vehicles entering Malaysia. Register at vep.jpj.gov.my if not done. The RFID tag must be fitted and active.
  • Motor insurance: most SG comprehensive policies include coverage for West Malaysia and Peninsular Malaysia ; check your policy wording. Confirm it covers EV-specific incidents.
  • Charging port: your IM uses CCS2 — this is the standard for all Malaysian public DC fast chargers. No adapter needed.
  • Malaysia charging app: download JomCharge (by EV Connection) before you cross the border. It is Malaysia's largest CCS2 DC network with 1,200+ charging points.
  • SP App cross-border roaming: since Oct 2025, the SP App connects to selected JomCharge chargers in Malaysia; you can locate, start, and pay for JomCharge sessions via the SP App. Bills in SGD with a 5% platform fee. ChargeUP rewards do not apply in Malaysia.
  • ADAS note: speed limit display may show incorrect limits in Malaysia; drive to posted road signs at all times.
  • IM app connectivity: should work in Malaysia via roaming eSIM but some connected services may be limited outside SG.
How do I plan my charging stops for a SG to KL drive? ▾
The Singapore to KL drive is approximately 350 km via the North-South Expressway (NSE / Plus Highway). With the 75 kWh (Cat A) variant expect real-world range of approximately 280–340 km depending on speed and A/C load. For the 100 kWh variants expect approximately 380–450 km.

Recommended charge stop strategy (75 kWh variant):
  • Charge to at least 80–90% before leaving SG.
  • First stop: Ayer Keroh / Melaka R&R (approximately 210 km). DC chargers available via JomCharge. Top up to 80%.
  • Arrive KL with comfortable buffer (20%+).

For 100 kWh variants: SG to KL is achievable in one charge if you depart at 80%+ — but one top-up stop is recommended for peace of mind.

Key charging locations along the NSE (all CCS2 available via JomCharge):
  • Yong Peng R&R (~145 km from SG)
  • Ayer Keroh R&R / Melaka (~210 km)
  • Nilai / Seremban area (~310 km)
  • KL city (abundant: Pavilion KL, Mid Valley, KLCC vicinity, IOI City Mall Putrajaya)

Planning tip: Use JomCharge app or PlugShare to check live charger availability before departing each stop. Charger downtime does occur; always have a backup option mapped.
How do I plan charging for SG to Penang? ▾
SG to Penang is approximately 680 km — a full-day drive requiring at least 2–3 charge stops regardless of variant.

The NSE North-South Expressway is well-covered with DC fast chargers every 50–80 km via JomCharge, Gentari (Petronas), and ChargEV at major rest stops.

Suggested charge stops (75 kWh variant, charge to ~80% each stop):
  • Stop 1: Yong Peng R&R (~145 km from SG)
  • Stop 2: Tapah R&R (~320 km) — important buffer stop. Pro tip: even if you have 40–50% here, top up — weekend queues at Ipoh town chargers can be 60+ minutes.
  • Stop 3: Ipoh area (~380 km) or continue to Gurun/Sungai Petani (~530 km)
  • Arrive Georgetown / Penang (~680 km) — abundant charging at Gurney Plaza, Queensbay Mall, AEON Bayan Baru.

For 100 kWh variants: 2 charge stops are typically sufficient (Yong Peng or Ayer Keroh, then Tapah or Ipoh).

Penang is one of Malaysia's most EV-ready destinations with multiple CCS2 DC chargers across the island. Plan a slow overnight charge at your hotel if staying multiple nights.

What real-world range should I plan for on Malaysian highways? ▾
Malaysian highway speeds are typically 90–110 km/h (legal limit 110 km/h on NSE). At these speeds, with A/C running in tropical heat, expect:

  • 75 kWh (Cat A) variant: approximately 280–320 km real-world range from 100% to near empty. Plan stops every 200–250 km to maintain a 20%+ buffer.
  • 100 kWh Sport RWD variant: approximately 380–440 km real-world.
  • 100 kWh Performance AWD variant: approximately 320–380 km real-world (AWD system draws more power).

Key factors that reduce range on Malaysian roads:
  • Sustained highway speed (100–110 km/h) - more wind resistance than city driving.
  • Constant A/C load in 28–35°C ambient temperature - can reduce range 15–20%.
  • Full passenger load and luggage.
  • Hill sections (Titiwangsa Range between Rawang and Ipoh) - climb reduces range, descent partially recovers.

Safety rule: never let the battery drop below 20% on the highway. Charge stations can be busy, broken, or require a detour off the expressway.

Drive modes, regen & vehicle features 5 FAQs ▾
What driving modes are available and what does each do?Manual-sourced ▾
Per the IM6 manual — access via Settings > Driving on the centre screen:

  • Comfort Mode: combines economy, power and comfort. Meets the daily driving needs of users. Suitable for most SG daily driving.
  • Energy-saving Mode (ECO): optimises energy consumption for maximum range. Includes a Super Energy Saving option which extends range further by limiting intelligent drive, reducing A/C power, and turning off electrical appliances such as ambient lights, multimedia and displays.
  • Sport Mode: maximum power output, 4WD preferred (AWD variants). More responsive throttle and steering.
  • Custom/Personalised Mode*: allows individual tuning of power, braking, steering, suspension and other systems. (* check your variant)

WARNING per the manual: Switching the driving mode when the vehicle is in motion can divert the driver's attention from road conditions and may result in accidents. [Manual-sourced]
How do I adjust energy recovery (regen braking) levels?Manual-sourced ▾
Per the IM manual — access via Settings > Driving > ERS:

  • OFF: no energy recovery during coasting — car coasts freely like a petrol car.
  • LOW: light regeneration, longer coast distance.
  • STANDARD: stronger regeneration, shorter coast distance — closest to one-pedal feel (but does NOT bring the car to a complete stop).

Note: regen does not activate when the battery is fully charged or if the battery temperature is too high or low. Coasting in N gear disables regen. The manual recommends against coasting in N gear while driving.
What is Auto Hold and when should I use it?Manual-sourced ▾
Per the IM6 manual — Auto Hold is useful in SG's frequent stop-start traffic and on slopes at traffic lights:

HOW IT WORKS:
Auto Hold has three states:
1. Standby: With the driver's seat belt fastened, the driver's door closed, and the vehicle in READY state — press the Auto Hold button on the central screen to enter Standby state.
2. Operating (Parking state): When moving forward, press the brake pedal to stop the vehicle, then firmly press the brake pedal again to switch Auto Hold from Standby to Parking state. The car stays stationary and you can remove your foot from the pedal. When you press the accelerator to move off, Auto Hold releases automatically (based on slope and gear selected).
3. To disable: Press the Auto Hold button on the central screen again.

Note: If the vehicle is stopped by firmly pressing the brake pedal, Auto Hold will directly enter Parking state.

AUTO HOLD EXITS the parking state when:
  • The EPB switch is pressed with the brake pedal pressed.
  • The seat belt is released.
  • The power system is turned off.
  • The vehicle remains static for a length of time.
  • The Auto Hold switch is touched again.
IMPORTANT: When Auto Hold exits due to any of the above, the EPB is automatically applied. [Manual-sourced]

CAR WASH WARNING per the manual: The Auto Hold MUST be switched off before entering an automatic car wash — otherwise the EPB may suddenly apply and cause vehicle damage. [Manual-sourced]

Driver Welcome Function: when enabled (Settings > Driver Convenience) per the manual:
  • Exiting: When you park in P gear, AND release the seat belt, AND open the door — the seat automatically slides back to give you more space to get out. All three conditions must be met. [Manual-sourced]
  • Entering: Unlock the vehicle, open the door, get in. When the door is closed OR the brake pedal is pressed (whichever comes first), the seat automatically moves forward to your last memorised position. [Manual-sourced]
What is Crab Mode and how do I use it?Manual-sourced* Check your variant ▾
Crab Mode uses the rear-wheel steering system so that the rear wheels turn in the SAME direction as the front wheels (instead of opposite), allowing the car to move diagonally at low speeds. Useful for precise positioning in tight parking situations.

The rear-wheel steering system is always active (it automatically adjusts at different speeds for better manoeuvrability at low speed and stability at high speed). Crab mode is a specific setting where this behaviour is exaggerated for lateral movement.

One Touch Park — parking guide & checklist 7 FAQs ▾
What are all the One Touch parking features on my IM?Manual-sourced ▾
There are four distinct One Touch intelligent parking features, all accessible via the One Touch iAD menu on the centre screen:

1. One Touch Park (APA) - automatic parking-in
2. One Touch Escape - automatic parking-out
3. One Touch Side (Curbside) - lateral sideways nudge into position
4. One Touch Reverse (Tracking) - auto-reverse along remembered path

All four are also accessible via the HOME interface and the 360 surround-view interface. In all four functions the system controls steering and speed; the driver remains responsible for safety and must be ready to brake immediately at all times.
One Touch Park (parking-in) — what it supports and how to use itManual-sourced ▾
WHAT IT SUPPORTS:
  • Perpendicular and parallel parking slots with clear marking lines.
  • Unoccupied spaces beside any car (spatial parking without marking lines).
  • With good lighting and clear marking lines, slot detection works without reference objects on both sides.

ACTIVATION (drive below 20 km/h):
1. Enable via 'One Touch Parking' on the HOME interface, OR engage R gear (if Quick One Touch Parking = On in APA Settings), OR tap 'Park' on the 360 or iAD interface.
2. System scans for a slot. When found, brake to stop safely, select the slot on screen and confirm.
3. After tapping 'Start Parking' — do NOT release the brake pedal until the system prompts 'Please Release Brake'.
4. System completes parking and prompts 'Successful Parking'.

Note: If the vehicle travels out of range after a slot is found, the system will scan again.

INTERRUPTION AND RESUME:
  • Touching the steering wheel or changing gear: system exits immediately.
  • Opening a door mid-park: system pauses -close the door and resume via the centre screen.
  • Braking during the manoeuvre: system pauses - release the brake and resume via the centre screen.
  • If speed exceeds 25 km/h during search: system returns to APA main interface.
Silent Search — how the car finds a parking space automatically while you driveManual-sourced ▾
Silent Search runs passively in the background with no input needed. When driving in D gear below 15 km/h in a car park or roadside area, the system silently scans for nearby spaces. A parking space icon appears on the instrument screen when a suitable space is found.

Note: The icon only appears when the space meets the full APA dimensional and positional requirements. Not every space you see will trigger the icon.

IN R GEAR: Silent Search also works when reversing below 8 km/h, but in R gear it only detects perpendicular spaces (with or without marking lines) — not parallel spaces.

LIMITATIONS per the manual:
  • Does not work on ramps.
  • Exits automatically if speed exceeds 15 km/h.
  • Cannot run simultaneously with other intelligent driving assistance functions.
  • Opening any door, bonnet, or boot causes it to exit.
  • Dark environments or adverse weather significantly reduce detection capability.
One Touch Escape (parking-out) - how to use it ▾
One Touch Escape automatically steers the vehicle out of a parking space to the exit channel. Available after APA parking-in OR after manual parking, provided the vehicle has not moved from its parked position.

SPACE REQUIREMENTS:
  • Parallel space: at least 1.1 m of clear space in front and behind the vehicle.
  • Perpendicular space: at least 0.6 m of clear space on both sides of the vehicle.
  • For manually parked vehicles: the rear of the vehicle must have passed through the adjacent parking space during entry for Escape to work.

HOW TO USE:
1. Enable via 'Pull Out' on the 360 interface, HOME interface, or iAD menu — OR say 'I want to park out' via voice activation.
2. System detects and displays available exit directions (front-left, front-right). Select your preferred direction.
3. System displays the path range — confirm there are no obstacles in that path.
4. System controls steering and speed to exit. Monitor surroundings at all times.
5. On completion, vehicle automatically engages P gear and prompts 'Parking Out Completed'.

PAUSE AND RESUME:
  • Tap [Pause], brake, or open a door — system pauses. Tap [Continue] to resume.
  • System exits permanently if paused more than 1 minute due to obstacles, or if the driver touches the steering wheel or changes gear. answer here.
One Touch Side (Curbside) - nudging the car sideways into position ▾
One Touch Side (labelled 'Curbside' on screen) nudges the car sideways in 15 cm steps toward a kerb or wall. Useful for precise positioning in tight SG car parks after you've parked, without needing to reverse and re-park.

HOW IT WORKS:
  • Vehicle must be stationary in P gear or Auto Hold mode before enabling.
  • Moves the vehicle laterally left or right in 15 cm increments per activation.
  • If a reference object (kerb or wall) is present and the car is roughly parallel to it (within about 18 degrees at the rear or 6 degrees at the front), the system aligns the vehicle to it.
  • If no reference object is detected, it simply shifts the vehicle 15 cm in the chosen direction.

RECOMMENDED STARTING CONDITIONS per the manual:
  • At least 20 cm from a kerb; at least 40 cm from a wall.
  • At least 1.5 m total clear space (front gap + rear gap combined).
  • Body should be as parallel to the reference object as possible before starting.

ENABLE: Tap 'Curbside' on the 360 interface, HOME interface, or iAD menu.

Do NOT use if a ball hitch, bike rack, or trailer is attached. Not recommended on slopes above 5 degrees, potholes, or in poor lighting or adverse weather.Enter answer here.
One Touch Reverse (Tracking) - auto-reversing back out of a tight spot ▾
One Touch Reverse (labelled 'Reverse' or 'Tracking' on screen) silently records your recent driving path and automatically reverses the car back along that path. Useful when you've driven into a dead-end, narrow lane, or difficult situation and need to reverse out the way you came.

HOW IT WORKS:
  • The system silently memorises your last driven path up to 50 m while driving at 0–25 km/h — no input required. It records continuously in the background.
  • When you need to reverse out, enable the function and it automatically steers and controls speed to reverse along the memorised path.

REQUIREMENTS per the manual:
  • Minimum 1 m of valid memorised path must have been recorded.
  • Road width must be greater than 3 m — function exits if road becomes narrower.

IMPORTANT LIMITATIONS:
  • Path recording pauses when another intelligent driving assistance system is active.
  • There will be some deviation from the exact original path — monitor surroundings throughout.
  • Sensors cannot identify hanging obstacles, barbed wire, guardrails, or fences.
  • Obstacles within 30 cm of the body are in the sensor blind spot — do not enable if obstacles are this close.
  • Does not work in dark environments, adverse weather, or on roads with cliffs or height differences.

HOW TO ENABLE: Tap 'Reverse' on the 360 interface, HOME interface, or iAD menu. Stop the vehicle, keep braking, then tap 'Reverse' to start. Remaining tracking distance is shown in real time on screen.
Pre-use safety checklist - applies to ALL One Touch parking features ▾
Vehicle & environment prep (all four functions):

1. No attachments: Do not use any One Touch function if a ball hitch, bike rack, or trailer is attached to the tow hitch. [Manual-verified]
2. Conditions: Good lighting, suitable weather, flat ground — no steep slopes.
3. Unfold mirrors: Exterior mirrors must be unfolded so side cameras function correctly.
4. Clean cameras: Front, rear, and side cameras must be clean and unobstructed.
5. All doors, bonnet, and boot must be closed before starting.

PHASE 2 — For One Touch Park specifically:
6. Drive close to parking spaces to help the system scan effectively.
7. Verify the lines: Standard perpendicular or parallel slots only. AVOID: U-shaped lines, dot lines, diagonal/irregular lines, grassland, colour-coded slots, mechanical/automated garage systems.
8. Check the width: Avoid spots narrower than approximately 2.75 m.
9. Confirm legality: System CANNOT determine if a space is legal. You must confirm before starting.

PHASE 3 — During any manoeuvre:
10. Stay ready to brake at all times — driver remains fully responsible.
11. Watch for sensor blind spots: low curbs, thin poles, overhanging branches, obstacles within 30 cm of the body.
12. Roof rack warning: Watch for overhead obstructions such as A/C ventilation pipes that sensors cannot detect.
13. Use parking radar audio feedback to judge distance to obstacles.
14. Take over immediately if a pedestrian, cyclist, or vehicle appears suddenly — the system may not brake in time.
15. Final adjustment: System may complete the manoeuvre earlier than expected — adjust the final position manually if needed.
Long-term care, storage & emergencies 5 FAQs ▾
What is the running-in advice for a new IM?Manual-sourced ▾
Per the IM manual:

  • For the first 1,500 km: avoid rapid acceleration or emergency braking to allow brake pads and tyres to bed in properly. [Manual-sourced]
  • During running-in, drive smoothly and progressively - this helps long-term brake and tyre performance.

Note: EVs have fewer moving parts to bed in compared to ICE vehicles, but the brakes and tyres still benefit from a gradual run-in period.
How do I prepare my IM if parking it for a long period (holiday, etc.)?Manual-sourced ▾
Per the IM manual:

  • Charge the high-voltage battery to at least 50% SOC before leaving.
  • Charge at least once every 3 months — keep above 50% on the instrument after each charge.
  • NEVER leave the car for more than 7 days with a critically low battery (no valid driving range shown on the instrument); this can permanently damage the battery cells.
  • If the 12V auxiliary battery will be unused for more than 1 month, the manual recommends removing the negative cable from the 12V battery to prevent drain.
  • After a long storage period: perform a complete equalisation charge (slow AC charge to 100%) before returning to regular driving.
My 12V battery is flat and the car won't start. How do I jump-start it?Manual-sourced ▾
Per the IM6 manual (jump-start the 12V battery only):

SAFETY WARNINGS per the manual:
  • NEVER start an IM by pushing or towing.
  • NEVER jump-start with a fuel vehicle's battery — may damage the IM Low Voltage Battery Management system (LVBM). [Manual-sourced]
  • Jumper cables must be APPROVED FOR USE with 12V car batteries — not just any cables. [Manual-sourced]
  • Ensure there is NO NAKED FIRE in the front compartment. [Manual-sourced]
  • Ensure jumper cables are securely connected and do NOT touch each other or other moving parts — otherwise sparking could lead to fire or explosion. [Manual-sourced]

Procedure:
1. Ensure both vehicles are powered off and all electrics turned off.
2. Both batteries must be 12V — same rated voltage. Use jumper cables approved for 12V batteries.
3. Connect the RED jumper cable: positive (+) of donor battery to positive (+) of disabled vehicle. If the tailgate cannot be opened, use the positive jumper point in the front compartment fuse box.
4. Connect the BLACK jumper cable: negative (-) of donor battery to a good unpainted earth point on the disabled vehicle (crossbeam or similar — NOT directly to the battery, and keep it well away from the battery and bypass the brake lines). [Manual-sourced]
5. Start the donor vehicle and run for a few minutes.
6. Attempt to start the disabled IM. If the disabled vehicle will not start after several attempts, it may need to be repaired — contact EK for an overhaul.
7. After both vehicles start, allow the disabled vehicle to run for more than 30 minutes BEFORE turning off the donor vehicle and disconnecting the cables. It is recommended to turn off comfort appliances such as lights and air conditioning after starting, and keep the vehicle running for 1 to 2 hours to restore the battery power fully. [Manual-sourced]
8. Disconnect in EXACT REVERSE order: BLACK from earth point on disabled vehicle FIRST, then rest.

Do NOT switch on any electrics on the disabled vehicle before removing the jumper cables. [Manual-sourced]
My IM broke down on the road. Can I be towed?Manual-sourced ▾
CRITICAL: DO NOT tow an IM with all four wheels on the ground. The electric drive unit will be damaged. [Manual-sourced]

Only these methods are permitted per the manual:
  • Suspended towing (flatbed / vehicle transporter): preferred method. All wheels off the ground.
  • Auxiliary wheels: if suspended towing is not available, auxiliary wheels must keep the drive wheels off the ground.
  • Pushing: only permissible at speeds below 5 km/h for a maximum of 3 minutes — e.g., to clear a junction. [Manual-sourced]

TOWING EYES: The car has two towing EYES (sockets) — one at the front and one at the rear of the vehicle. There is ONE tow hook stored in the trunk that screws into these sockets as needed. To fit: remove the small cover from the bumper, then screw the tow hook fully into the threaded hole in the bumper beam. [Manual-sourced]

IMPORTANT per the manual: These towing eyes are for towing YOUR OWN IM onto a transporter ONLY — they are NOT designed for towing other vehicles. A hard tow rod is recommended; a soft rope may be used if a hard rod is not available. [Manual-sourced]

When being towed: insert the driver's seat belt into the buckle, set the shift lever to N, and release the EPB. Turn on hazard warning lamps. No passengers should remain in the vehicle being towed. [Manual-sourced]

Contact EK or your roadside assistance immediately to arrange flatbed recovery.
Can I take my IM through an automatic car wash?Manual-sourced ▾
You can use automatic car wash equipment, BUT you MUST switch off Auto Hold before entering.

WARNING: if Auto Hold is active when entering a car wash, the Electronic Parking Brake (EPB) may suddenly apply, causing potential vehicle damage and safety hazard.

Additional tips from the manual:
  • After a car wash, check that camera lenses (front, side, rear) are clean and unobstructed before using parking assist or ADAS features.
  • High-pressure washers should not be directed at camera lenses, sensors, or seal areas.

Why does my displayed range vary so much? What is DFRE / WLTP/ CLTC?WishlistAwaiting EK ▾
The range figure displayed is a dynamic estimate that changes based on recent driving behaviour, A/C load, speed, and temperature — not a fixed number (W-010 / IM-026).

Rating standards explained:
  • WLTP (Worldwide Harmonised Light Vehicle Test Procedure): closer to real-world but still measured in ideal conditions.
  • DFRE (Dynamic Fuel/Driving Range Estimate) is your car's real-time prediction based on immediate driving conditions
  • CLTC (Chinese Light-duty Vehicle Test Cycle): the test cycle used for China-market IM specs. More optimistic than real-world SG driving — typically plan for 75–85% of CLTC rating.

In SG conditions, the main range reducers are: constant A/C (15–20% reduction), sustained highway speed, full passenger load, and accessories. DFRE is generally much more reliable for day-to-day travel, as it actively penalizes energy-heavy habits like highway speeds or glass roof heat-soak. WLTP will usually over-predict your range.

Note: IM App only displays WLTP figures currently even though car settings may be set to DFRE

Owners have asked EK to clarify the specific range calculation method used and to add a plain-language explanation in the app. Status: pending.
